❄️

Winter

  • Parks are quieter with far fewer visitors — great for dogs that prefer less stimulation
  • Reduced daylight limits evening visits
  • Snow and ice can make access difficult; some paths are cleared, others are not
  • Watch for salted sidewalks that irritate paw pads — consider dog boots or paw wax
  • Shorter walks recommended during extreme cold snaps
🌸

Spring

  • Mud season is the biggest challenge — sections or entire parks may close temporarily after thaws and rains
  • Increased risk of leptospirosis in wet, muddy terrain
  • Tick activity increases significantly — check your dog after every visit
  • Parks with concrete, gravel, or artificial turf surfaces handle spring much better than natural grass
Best spring parks (mud-free surfaces): Puptown (concrete/gravel), Logan Square Dog Park (concrete), Grant Bark Park (artificial turf), Mary Bartelme DFA (artificial turf), Wiggly Field (pea gravel)
☀️

Summer

  • Peak attendance at all parks — visit during off-peak hours (early morning or late evening) if your dog prefers less activity
  • Water features are active at many parks — kiddie pools, sprinklers, and splash areas
  • Montrose Dog Beach is at its best for swimming
  • Heat can be an issue at parks without shade — Walsh DFA is notably shadeless
  • Bring extra water for your dog, especially at parks without fountains
Parks with summer water features: Horner DFA, River DFA, Churchill DFA, Portage DFA, Fred Anderson DFA, Wiggly Field, Clarendon DFA, Puptown
🍂

Fall

  • Generally the best season for dog park visits
  • Moderate temperatures, lower crowds than summer, dry ground
  • Beautiful foliage at parks with mature trees (Wiggly Field, Churchill DFA, Horner DFA)
  • Great time to explore new parks before winter sets in

Surface Types & Weather

The surface type of a dog park significantly affects its usability across seasons:

Surface Mud Risk Example Parks
Artificial turf None Grant Bark Park, Mary Bartelme, Fred Anderson, Coliseum
Concrete / gravel None Puptown, Logan Square Dog Park
Pea gravel / sand Low Wiggly Field, Churchill DFA
Wood chips Medium Horner DFA
Natural grass High Edgewater Dog Park
